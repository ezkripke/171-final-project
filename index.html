How can we understand the steep increase in crime over this 40 year period?

1980-1988: Ronald Reagan's Presidency and the beginning of the "War on Drugs" - during which time, the US prison population doubled.
1986: Reagan passes the Anti-Drug Abuse act of 1986, creating stricter punishments for drug-based offences.
1994: President Bill Clinton passes the Crime Bill, increasing punishments for all types of prisonable offences and granting funding to federal prisons across the US.
2003: Congress creates, increases, or expands nearly 40 mandatory minimum sentences.
2008: President George Bush signs the Second Chance Act, offering funding for prison rehabilitation programs across the US.
2008-2016: Barack Obama's presidency - the first President in decades to decrease incarceration rates nation-wide.
